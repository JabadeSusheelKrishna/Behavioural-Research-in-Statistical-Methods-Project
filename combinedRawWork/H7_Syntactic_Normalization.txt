Hypothesis 7: Effect of Syntactic Normalization (Syntactic Transformations)

Methodology:
H7 tested whether transforming the syntactic structure of a sentence (e.g., from Active to Passive or vice-versa) between the encoding and retrieval stages incurs a memory penalty. Word Retrieval (WR) accuracy was compared for "Identity" trials (no change) vs. "Transformed" trials using a Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.

Statistical Results:
- Test Statistic: Wilcoxon W = 4521.0
- p-value: p = 6.597e-07
- Effect Size: Rank-biserial correlation r_rb = 0.536 (Large effect)
- WR Accuracy (Identity): 0.811
- WR Accuracy (Transformed): 0.683

Interpretation:
The results reveal a highly significant "Syntactic Normalization" penalty of 12.8%. Participants were significantly less likely to correctly retrieve a word if the grammatical voice of the sentence had been changed since they first saw it. This demonstrates that although the semantic meaning remains constant, the brain encodes the specific syntactic structure as part of the episodic memory trace, and mismatching that structure during retrieval hinders access to the encoded information.
