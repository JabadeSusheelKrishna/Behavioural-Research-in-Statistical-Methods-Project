Hypothesis 8: Memory Strength Model (Continuum of Retrieval)

Methodology:
H8 tested the "Memory Strength Model," proposing that the speed of sentence recognition (IR RT) during the recognition phase predicts the accuracy of subsequent word recall (WR Accuracy). A Generalized Estimating Equation (GEE) was used to model the binary outcome of WR Accuracy based on the continuous predictor of within-subject z-scored IR RT, while controlling for sentence condition and voice.

Statistical Results:
- GEE Coefficient (Beta): β = -0.0610 (standard error 0.0357)
- z-score: -1.71
- p-value: p = 0.088
- Odds Ratio (OR): 0.941

Interpretation:
The findings show a trending inverse relationship support for the memory strength model. Although not reaching the standard p < 0.05 threshold, the negative coefficient and Odds Ratio of 0.94 suggest that faster recognition decisions (during IR) are marginally associated with a higher probability of successful cued recall later. This aligns with the theoretical view that "fluent" or fast recognition is an index of a high-fidelity memory trace that is also more accessible for productive recall.
