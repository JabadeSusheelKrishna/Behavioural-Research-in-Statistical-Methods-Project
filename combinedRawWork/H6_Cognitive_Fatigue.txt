Hypothesis 6: Effect of Cognitive Fatigue on Experimental Performance

Methodology:
To assess whether participant performance declined over the course of the experiment, accuracy and reaction time were compared across three sequential blocks. A Friedman Test was used for the omnibus comparison, followed by Wilcoxon post-hoc tests.

Statistical Results (Accuracy):
- Omnibus Test (Friedman): χ²(2) = 88.484, p < 0.0001
- Effect Size: Kendall's W = 0.385 (Moderate)
- Block 1 Mean: 0.81
- Block 3 Mean: 0.61
- Post-hoc (Block 1 vs 3): W = 868.5, p < 0.0001, r = 0.654 (Large effect)

Statistical Results (Reaction Time):
- Omnibus Test (Friedman): χ²(2) = 9.814, p = 0.0074
- Effect Size: Kendall's W = 0.04 (Small)
- Median difference: RT increased (became slower) by approximately 110ms from Block 1 to 3.

Interpretation:
H6 is strongly supported, specifically for the accuracy measure. There is a clear and statistically significant decline in recognition accuracy as the experiment progresses, with performance dropping from ~81% to ~61%. This suggests that cognitive fatigue and a depletion of attentional resources significantly impact memory retrieval efficiency over time.
