Hypothesis 3: Effect of Word Memorability Type on Word Retrieval (WR) Accuracy

Methodology:
To examine whether the memorability of the cue words affects the accuracy of cued recall (Word Retrieval), a Friedman Test was applied to the WR accuracy scores across the four word-type conditions (HH, HL, LH, LL) for N = 112 participants.

Statistical Results:
- Omnibus Test (Friedman): χ²(3, N = 112) = 3.341, p = 0.3426
- Effect Size: Kendall’s W = 0.01

Interpretation:
The results do not support a significant effect of word type on WR accuracy. Unlike the CMS (recognition) metric, which showed sensitivity to noun memorability, the accuracy of retrieving a specific word when cued did not vary significantly between conditions. This suggests that while high-memorability nouns help participants remember "that they saw a sentence," they do not necessarily improve the "precision" of recalling the specific lexical items once the sentence context is activated, possibly due to ceiling effects in the recall task.
