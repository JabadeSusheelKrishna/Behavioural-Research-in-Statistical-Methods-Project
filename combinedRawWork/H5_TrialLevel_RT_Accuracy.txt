Hypothesis 5: Trial-Level Relationship Between Reaction Time and Accuracy

Methodology:
To investigate the binary outcome of accuracy (Correct/Incorrect) relative to retrieval speed, a Generalized Estimating Equation (GEE) / Logistic Regression approach was used on trial-level data. The primary predictor was within-subject z-scored Reaction Time (RT).

Statistical Results:
- Logistic Regression Coefficient (Beta): β = -0.8120
- Odds Ratio (OR): 0.4439 (95% CI: 0.3801 - 0.5186)
- p-value: p < 0.001
- Biserial Correlation: r = -0.3421

Interpretation:
The findings reveal a strong and highly significant inverse relationship between RT and Accuracy. Specifically, an Odds Ratio of 0.44 indicates that for every standard deviation increase in Reaction Time (slower response), the odds of the response being accurate decrease by more than half. This confirms a **Reversed Speed-Accuracy Trade-off** (also known as the "Decisiveness Effect"), where faster responses are systematically more accurate in recognition tasks, likely reflecting higher "memory strength" at the time of retrieval.
